## üåë Melatonin Agonists (Non-Controlled)
*Targets MT1 (Sleep onset) and MT2 (Circadian timing) receptors.*

**Ramelteon (Rozerem):**
* **Structure:** An indane core (mimics indole of melatonin).
* **Metabolism:** **CYP1A2** is the primary enzyme. 
* **DDI Warning:** Avoid with **Fluvoxamine** (potent 1A2 inhibitor) ‚Üí massive ‚Üë in drug levels.

## ü¶¥ Orexin Receptor Antagonists (DORAs)
*Mechanism: Shuts off the "Wake Switch" rather than turning on the "Sleep Switch."*

| Drug | Half-Life ($t_{1/2}$) | Clinical Pearl |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Suvorexant** | ~12 hours | Risk of next-day somnolence |
| **Lemborexant** | ~17-19 hours | High affinity for OX2R |
| **Daridorexant** | **~8 hours** | **Best safety profile** for next-day alertness |

## üî• Barbiturates: The High-Risk Class
*The "Displaced" Hypnotics.*


**The SAR "Hard" Rules:**
1. **5,5-Disubstitution:** Required for activity (prevents tautomerization).
2. **Lipophilicity:** Increasing the carbon chain at C5 increases potency but *decreases* duration.
3. **Thio-Barbiturates (C2 Oxygen $\rightarrow$ Sulfur):** Results in ultra-fast onset (e.g., Thiopental for anesthesia).

| Feature | Barbiturates | BZDs / Z-Drugs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
